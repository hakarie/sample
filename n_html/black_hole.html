EXPLORING BLACK HOLES

What are Black Holes?

A black hole is a region in space where the gravitational pull is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape from it. They are formed when massive stars collapse under their own gravity.

Properties of Black Holes

  • Event Horizon: The boundary beyond which nothing can escape the gravitational pull of a black hole.
  • Singularity: A point of infinite density at the center of a black hole.
  • Spaghettification: The process where tidal forces near a black hole stretch objects into long thin shapes.

Types of Black Holes

  • Stellar Black Holes: Formed from the collapse of massive stars.
  • Supermassive Black Holes: Found at the centers of most galaxies and can have masses millions to billions of times that of the Sun.
  • Intermediate Black Holes: With masses between stellar and supermassive black holes.

Research and Discoveries

Scientists continue to study black holes using telescopes and other instruments. Discoveries such as gravitational waves have provided new insights into their behavior and the universe as a whole.
